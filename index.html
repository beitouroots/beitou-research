💾 Tech

SK Hynix Raises $26.5 Billion. The Biggest Foreign IPO in US History Trades Today

  • The tea: SK Hynix priced 177.9 million ADRs at $149 apiece, raising about $26.5 billion. That beats Alibaba's $25 billion from 2014 as the largest US IPO ever by a foreign company, and it is the biggest ADR offering on record. 🏆
  • The demand: the book was reported at more than seven times oversubscribed, with names like Baillie Gifford and Coatue indicating interest. Into a choppy chip tape, the memory king asked for a record and got it. 💪
  • The mechanics: the ADRs start conditional trading on the Nasdaq today under SKHYV, then switch to the regular SKHY ticker on Monday, July 13. Today's tape is the referendum. 🎯
  • Why it matters: the company says the money goes to AI-memory capacity: the first fab at the Yongin cluster, a new advanced packaging fab in Cheongju, and EUV lithography gear. This is not a cash-out, it is a war chest. 🍾

🧠 Deep Thoughts

Memory Is the New Oil: The $26.5 Billion Tell

  • The big idea: follow the money, not the narrative. SK Hynix did not raise $26.5 billion to buy back stock. Every stated dollar goes to capacity: the Yongin fab, advanced packaging in Cheongju, EUV machines. Companies only build like that when they can see demand years out. 🏗️
  • The chokepoint: AI models are increasingly memory-bound, not compute-bound. High-bandwidth memory is the scarce input every accelerator needs more of, and the suppliers who can make it at scale form a tiny club. Scarce input plus structural demand is the oldest setup in the book. 🛢️
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, watch the memory complex as its own asset class: HBM pricing, Micron (MU), the new SKHY ticker after Monday's switch, and the equipment makers who sell them the shovels. A hiking Fed can bruise the multiple; it cannot cancel the buildout. ⚙️
  • TL;DR: yesterday we said own the toll collectors of the AI boom. Today the biggest toll collector in memory listed on the Nasdaq and handed you a live quote. The tell is not the pop or the fade today. It is whether that capex keeps landing. 🔭

💥 The Standout

Micron Just Promised a Quarter-Trillion Dollars to American Silicon

  • The move: Micron said it will invest more than $250 billion in the US through 2035, up from the $200 billion plan it announced just last June, to feed AI-era memory demand. The stock closed up 7.5% and has gained more than 200% this year. When a memory company raises its own capex bar by fifty billion dollars, that is a demand statement, not a press release. 💾
  • The build: the centerpiece is the Clay, New York megafab campus, up to four fabs, which Micron expects to become the largest semiconductor manufacturing site in the US and the largest private investment in New York state history: roughly 50,000 New York jobs, including 9,000 direct Micron roles. First concrete was poured this week. The stated goal: make 40% of its DRAM in the United States. 🏗️
  • The supply chain: the plan folds in $3 billion for the US semiconductor supply chain, including $500 million toward GlobalWafers' 300-millimeter silicon wafer plant in Sherman, Texas. On the same day SK Hynix prices its own Nasdaq listing, the memory arms race went fully domestic. 🇺🇸

🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Market Just Told You It Prices Capex, Not Conflict

  • The takeaway: ninety targets were struck in Iran overnight and every major index closed green while crude fell 2%. That is not complacency; it is a market drawing a line between headlines and barrels. Until Hormuz supply is actually impaired, war risk trades as a day rental, and today the mediators in Qatar and Pakistan mattered more than the missiles. 🗓️
  • The signal: follow the money that got committed today, not the money that got scared. Micron pledged $250 billion, the Pentagon handed a laser maker a $627 million ceiling, and SK Hynix moved to price a $29 billion New York listing, all in one session. Physical-world capex, memory, defense, and power, is where the earnings growth is being manufactured for the next decade. ⚖️
  • The macro: the 6-to-12-month tension is that this capex boom is inflationary at the margin while the Fed is openly split, with a hike camp forming. The 10-year at 4.58% says the bond market has not picked a side yet. If oil stays tame, the capex trade runs; if Hormuz actually closes, everything above gets repriced in an afternoon. Position for the first, respect the second. 🌊
  • TL;DR: keep the barbell: the toll collectors of the AI and defense buildout on one end, cash-generating quality on the other. Earnings season referees it starting tomorrow: Delta before the bell, Hynix at the opening cross. The tape votes on the whole thesis in about 18 hours. 🔒
