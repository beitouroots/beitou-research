💻 Tech Earnings

Alphabet and Tesla Both Beat on Revenue, Get Sold Anyway as AI Spending Fears Take Over

  • The tea: Alphabet posted Q2 revenue of $119.8 billion, up 24% year-over-year and above the $117 billion analysts expected, with Google Cloud revenue up 82% to $24.8 billion, its fastest growth in years. ☁️
  • The catch: Alphabet raised its full-year 2026 capex guidance to $195-205 billion (from $180-190 billion) and flagged another jump in 2027, sending Class A shares (GOOGL) down about 4.5% pre-market, while Class C (GOOG) traded around $330, down about 3.3%. 📉
  • Tesla's story: Tesla delivered a record 480,126 vehicles, up 25%, but posted its first negative free cash flow in more than two years (a $1.09 billion deficit) as capex surged 142% to $5.79 billion on AI, robotics and Optimus; TSLA fell about 5.7% pre-market to around $352. 🔋
  • Why it matters: two AI-spending beats, two stock drops, Wall Street just showed it will punish more capex even when the growth numbers are real, exactly the tension yesterday's Big Brain flagged (today's Big Brain). 🔍
