🚨 Geopolitics

US Strikes Iran for a Ninth Straight Night, but Tehran Signals It's Open to Talks

  • The tea: US Central Command carried out a ninth consecutive night of strikes on Iran early Monday, with President Trump saying the US hit Iran "very hard again." A third American service member has died, this one in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone. 🚨
  • The twist: despite the fresh strikes, Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmail Baghaei said Tehran has received proposals from international mediators about resuming talks with the US, even while vowing Iran will keep defending itself "resolutely." 🕊️
  • The oil whiplash: Brent crude briefly topped $90-91 a barrel over the weekend on the escalation, then pulled back toward the high $80s Monday morning as the mediation talk gave traders some hope for de-escalation. 🛢️
  • Why it matters: analysts say markets are "distinguishing between elevated geopolitical risk and the likelihood of a wider systemic shock," treating this as an energy-and-inflation story for now, not a broad risk-off panic. ⚖️

🏛️ The Fed

The Fed's Own Inflation Math Is Flashing a Warning, and Waller Says He's Watching Closely

  • The setup: the FOMC meets July 28-29, the second meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh, who has called prices "too high" and declined to signal a cut; market pricing for a hike (rather than a cut) has been climbing in recent weeks. 📊
  • The primary source: in a July 13 speech, "Monetary Policy at a Crossroads," Fed Governor Christopher Waller said core PCE inflation climbed from 3% in December to 3.4% in May, and warned, "If we get another hot reading on core inflation this week, then the FOMC will need to consider tightening monetary policy in the near term." 🎙️
  • The read: outside analysts tracking the Fed's own forecasting inputs flag that core PCE is barely budging even as falling oil prices cool headline inflation, evidence some read as the Iran-war energy shock spreading into broader prices. 🔬
  • Why it matters: a hike, not a cut, is now a live conversation at the most AI-levered, richly valued stock market in a generation, and a chunk of the AI buildout is financed with debt that gets pricier if the Fed tightens. ⚖️
Sources Federal Reserve · Waller Speech The Motley Fool CNBC

Big Tech's Earnings Gauntlet Kicks Off, With Tesla, Alphabet, Intel and IBM All Reporting This Week

  • The tea: Wall Street has raised its expectations for Tesla (TSLA), Alphabet (GOOGL), Intel (INTC) and IBM (IBM), all due to report later this week, as investors look for proof the AI buildout is being monetized, not just spent on. 📅

🍿 Markets

AMC Posts Its Best Quarter in 106 Years and Jumps About 16% in Premarket Trading

  • The tea: AMC Entertainment (AMC) reported Q2 revenue of $1.60 billion, up 14.2% year over year and roughly $130 million above Wall Street's estimate, with adjusted EBITDA of $321.4 million, up 69.6%, the highest quarterly revenue and adjusted EBITDA in the chain's 106-year history, per its SEC filing. 🎬
  • The swing: adjusted earnings came in at $0.14 a share versus a forecast $0.06 loss; on a GAAP basis AMC still posted an $11.4 million net loss (versus a $4.7 million loss a year ago), so the "profit" story here is on an adjusted basis. 🔍
  • The driver: six separate films each topped $75 million in domestic opening weekends, US theater attendance rose about 12% year over year, and shares jumped roughly 16% in premarket trading. 🍿
  • Why it matters: not everything is waiting on the Fed or the AI trade, consumers are still showing up for a good movie, and AMC's operating leverage is real when revenue grows. 📈

🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Market Is Threading Two Needles at Once: War-Driven Inflation and an AI Earnings Test

  • The big idea: two separate risks are converging on the same six-to-twelve-month window. The Iran war keeps threatening to push energy costs higher and spill into core inflation, which is exactly what has the Fed's own Governor Waller warning a rate hike, not a cut, could be needed by the July 28-29 meeting (Story 2). At the same time, this week's earnings from Tesla, Alphabet, Intel and IBM (Story 3) are the market's best chance yet to prove the AI buildout is paying for itself. 🔬
  • The tension: those two forces pull in opposite directions for the AI trade. A chunk of the AI buildout runs on debt, so a hawkish Fed makes that capital more expensive right as investors demand proof of AI monetization. Good earnings this week would offset a rate scare; a Fed hike would raise the bar those earnings have to clear. ⚖️
  • The trade: watch whether Tesla, Alphabet, Intel and IBM can show real AI revenue, not just AI spending, and watch whether next week's inflation data gives Waller his "hot reading" or the disinflation story he says is still plausible. Whichever breaks first likely sets the tone for Q3. 🔍
  • TL;DR: the Iran war is a live inflation risk, the Fed is no longer ruling out a hike, and Big Tech has one week to convince the market the AI trade still deserves its premium. 🔒

🧠 The Big Brain · the bottom line
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The AI Trade's Newest Threat Isn't Just Spending, It's Competition

  • The takeaway: for two years the AI trade's big worry was whether hyperscalers were spending too much. Today added a second worry: what if someone else builds it cheaper. A Chinese startup's open model landed the same week Western chip stocks slid into a bear market, and that's not a coincidence Wall Street is comfortable ignoring. 🔬
  • The tension: the war premium in oil and a cooling chip trade are both symptoms of the same thing, markets pricing in more uncertainty about what the next six months actually cost, whether that's a barrel of crude out of the Gulf or a training run out of a US data center. ⚖️
  • The trade: watch whether Tesla's July 22 report and the rest of earnings season can show AI monetization holding up under cheaper competition, and watch the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend for whether this oil spike is a blip or the start of a longer one. 🔍
  • TL;DR: it was a losing week for stocks and a winning one for oil, and both moves are pointing at the same question: is the premium the market's been paying for AI and for energy security about to get a real stress test. 🔒
