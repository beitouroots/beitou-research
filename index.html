🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Real AI Bottleneck Isn't Chips Anymore, It's Power

  • The big idea: the money is committed. Big-five hyperscaler capex is set to hit roughly $725 billion in 2026, about 64% above 2025, and Goldman pegs combined 2025-2027 spend near $1.15 trillion, more than double the prior three years. The AI buildout is not slowing. 💸
  • The shift: the binding constraint is moving from GPUs to electricity. Goldman flags a US data-center power shortfall topping 11 GW today, widening toward 45 GW by 2028, with a big chunk of planned 2026 capacity at risk of slipping on grid and construction bottlenecks. ⚡
  • The play: that re-rates the unglamorous picks-and-shovels, power generation, grid gear, transformers, cooling and nuclear or gas. The trade widens out from the chips to the things that actually energize them. 🔋
  • TL;DR: over the next 6 to 12 months, watch capex guidance and power deals more closely than chip launches. In this buildout, whoever controls the electrons controls the timeline. 🔒
