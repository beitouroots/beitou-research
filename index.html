🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

Priced for a Hike: Investing in the 85% Regime

  • The big idea: the market now puts 85.3% odds on a Fed hike by December, per CME FedWatch. Read that again. The 2026 conversation has flipped from "when do they cut" to "how many hikes," and when the rate regime flips, asset leadership flips with it. 🔄
  • The collision: two inflationary engines are running at once: an energy shock at the world's busiest oil chokepoint, and an AI capex supercycle that prices $28 billion listings above market and signs $19 billion single-tenant data-center leases. The Fed is being asked to tighten into a generational buildout. That has no clean playbook. ⚙️
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, own the toll collectors of both booms: energy producers and services, defense, the memory oligopoly, and the power and data-center landlords. Fund it from long-duration unprofitable growth and rate-sensitive small caps, the assets a hiking Fed punishes first. 📊
  • TL;DR: do not trade the last regime. If December hike odds hold north of 80%, the market's spine is real assets and cash flow today, and every dip in the toll collectors is a gift, not a warning. 🔒

🧠 The Big Brain · the bottom line
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Discount Rate Now Lives in the Strait of Hormuz

  • The takeaway: today's minutes confirmed what the tape already suspected: Warsh's Fed is genuinely split, with many participants seeing rates ending the year at or slightly below the current range and many others seeing them above it. A divided Fed does not decide; the data decides. And as of today, the most important data point in America is the price of a barrel. 🗓️
  • The signal: watch the 10-year, not the headlines. It rose to 4.59%, the highest since mid-May, on the same day stocks mostly held their ground. That is the bond market pricing energy-driven inflation risk while the equity market prices resilience. One of them gets to be right; historically, when oil is the driver, bonds usually see it first. ⚖️
  • The macro: the 6-to-12-month question is whether this is June all over again, a spike that faded to pre-war levels within weeks, or a regime change in the barrel. The difference this time: Iran's export license is revoked, so supply is actually impaired, not just threatened. If Brent holds the high $70s into August, the hike camp inside the Fed gets louder and the broadening trade needs energy hedges to survive. 🌊
  • TL;DR: keep the barbell: quality balance sheets on one end, energy exposure on the other, and let earnings season referee the rest. Delta opens the books tomorrow morning; the consumer's summer, in fuel-adjusted dollars, is about to testify. 🔒
