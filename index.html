🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Hormuz Premium Is Becoming a Permanent Line Item

  • The big idea: this is now a recurring show: strikes, a closure threat, an oil spike, a partial cool-down, repeat. When a chokepoint that carries about 20% of the world's oil gets threatened this often, the risk premium stops being an event and starts being a feature. Price it like a subscription, not a one-time charge. 🔁
  • The physics: the strait does not have to actually close to matter. Insurance, freight and rerouting costs rise on the threat alone, and every producer, refiner and utility starts paying up for supply security. That is structural demand for energy that never touches Hormuz. 🚢
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, watch the energy security complex: US LNG exporters, pipeline capacity that bypasses the strait, tanker rates, and plain old US shale optionality. In a world where the Fed's next move might be a hike, energy is quietly reclaiming its old job as portfolio insurance. 🛡️
  • TL;DR: Friday we said memory is the new oil. This weekend, oil stood up and reminded everyone it is still the old oil. Own some insurance. 🛢️
