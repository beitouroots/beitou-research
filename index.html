💾 Chips

Samsung Printed Tech's Biggest Quarter Ever. The Stock Tanked Anyway

  • The tea: Samsung's preliminary Q2 landed overnight: operating profit of 89.4 trillion won, about $58.4 billion, a 19-fold jump from a year ago on sales of about 171 trillion won.   • Why it matters: the memory supercycle is CONFIRMED, the numbers are real. What changed is the bar: beating is no longer enough when everyone already owns it. 💾

A Projectile Hit an LNG Tanker Right Where the Truce Lives

  • The tea: the Al Rekayyat, an LNG carrier owned by Qatar's state shipper Nakilat, was struck by a projectile early Tuesday about 8 nautical miles off Limah, Oman, while exiting the Strait of Hormuz, and caught fire. Security consultancy EOS Risk classifies it as a drone or missile strike. 🚨
  • The stakes: it is the first Qatari LNG tanker attacked since the war, and a direct stress test of the US-Iran agreement meant to keep the strait quiet. Early reporting points toward Iran; nothing official yet. 🕊️
  • The tape: Brent popped 1.32% to a one-week high near $72.94, WTI climbed 1.27% back above $69, and European natural gas jumped as much as 4.5% in early trade. 🔥
  • Why it matters: cheap crude has been the Fed's best friend all summer. If Hormuz risk re-prices oil, the "inflation risks have come down" pillar starts to wobble. Watch the follow-through, not the headline. 🦅

🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Market Stopped Paying for Beats and Started Policing Capex

  • The takeaway: put Monday and Tuesday side by side and you have the whole 2026 AI trade in two frames. Monday, a supplier's sales beat sent the Dow to a record. Tuesday, the best quarter in tech history got sold. The market is no longer paying for proof of demand; it is policing what the winners spend and who might undercut them next. 🗓️
  • The signal: the DeepSeek chip report matters more than its details. When your biggest customers start sketching their own inference silicon, the thing on trial is not AI demand, it is the scarcity premium baked into every chip multiple. Demand can stay historic while the toll rates get renegotiated. ⚖️
  • The macro: the friendly backdrop got a little less friendly. The Hormuz attack put a tail-risk premium back under oil, and firmer crude feeds exactly the inflation nerves that make a September hike livelier. Tomorrow's minutes from Warsh's first meeting tell you how twitchy the new Fed really is. 🌊
  • TL;DR: the 6-to-12-month bet stays with the broadening: small caps, rate-sensitives, and the other 490 stocks, with chip exposure sized for dispersion, not conviction. Rotation days like today are the broadening thesis cashing its first checks. 🔒
