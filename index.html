🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The AI Capex Bill Is Coming Due, and Higher-for-Longer Rates Make It Bigger

  • The big idea: the five biggest hyperscalers are on pace to spend over $600 billion on infrastructure this year, roughly 75% of it aimed at AI, and they're increasingly funding it with debt rather than pure cash flow, with well over $100 billion in tech-sector bond issuance in the past year alone. 💰
  • The math problem: analysts estimate the industry needs something like $500 billion a year in spend to justify roughly $2 trillion in eventual revenue, a return on capital nobody has proven out yet, and that math gets harder every time the 10-year (Story 4) or oil (Story 1) pushes borrowing and input costs higher. 🧮
  • The next 6-12 months: watch tonight's Alphabet and Tesla numbers, then next week's Meta, Microsoft, Amazon and Apple prints, for real evidence that cloud and AI revenue growth is catching up to the spend, because a Fed that isn't cutting removes the cheap-money assumption a lot of this capex boom was built on. 🔍
  • TL;DR: Big Tech has borrowed big to build the AI future, and a Fed that isn't cutting anytime soon means the market is about to find out whether that bet pays off on a real timeline, starting tonight. 🔒

💥 The Standout

A Chinese AI Model Just Designed a Working Chip, and Wall Street's Chip-Software Giants Are Still Feeling It

  • The feat: Chinese startup Moonshot AI's Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model, autonomously ran a full chip design flow in 48 hours using only open-source EDA tools, no licensed software from Cadence or Synopsys, producing a working chip on the freely available Nangate 45nm library. 🇨🇳
  • The market reaction: Cadence and Synopsys each tumbled roughly 9% when the news broke last week, erasing billions in combined market value, and both names remain among today's laggards as investors keep digesting the threat to their software moat. 📉
  • The catch: the demo ran on a 45nm process node, about four generations behind the 3nm and 2nm nodes where cutting-edge chip design actually happens, so the near-term threat to the high end of the business looks limited. 🔬
  • Why it matters: it's the same "cheaper AI just showed up" scare that rattled Nvidia and peers with DeepSeek, this time aimed at the software tools that make advanced chips possible in the first place. ⚖️
