💾 Chips

Samsung Printed Tech's Biggest Quarter Ever. The Stock Tanked Anyway

  • The tea: Samsung's preliminary Q2 landed overnight: operating profit of 89.4 trillion won, about $58.4 billion, a 19-fold jump from a year ago on sales of about 171 trillion won. That is the biggest quarterly operating profit ever reported by a tech company, past anything Nvidia or Apple has printed. 👑
  • The twist: the stock closed down 6.9% in Seoul anyway, after falling as much as 10% intraday, and dragged the KOSPI down 4.91%. Blame a monster run into the print, the stock is up nearly 150% this year, plus fresh worries about AI spending. Sell the news, textbook edition. 📉
  • The read-across: US chips caught the cold pre-market: Micron (MU) down more than 5% and Nvidia (NVDA) down 2.1%, with KLA, Marvell, Broadcom and AMD lower too. Nasdaq-100 futures are off 0.69%. 🥶
  • Why it matters: the memory supercycle is CONFIRMED, the numbers are real. What changed is the bar: beating is no longer enough when everyone already owns it. 💾

A Projectile Hit an LNG Tanker Right Where the Truce Lives

  • The tea: the Al Rekayyat, an LNG carrier owned by Qatar's state shipper Nakilat, was struck by a projectile early Tuesday about 8 nautical miles off Limah, Oman, while exiting the Strait of Hormuz, and caught fire. Security consultancy EOS Risk classifies it as a drone or missile strike. 🚨

  • Why it matters: cheap crude has been the Fed's best friend all summer. If Hormuz risk re-prices oil, the "inflation risks have come down" pillar starts to wobble. Watch the follow-through, not the headline. 🦅

🧠 Deep Thoughts

One Green Monday Is Not a Verdict, It Is an Opening Statement

  • The takeaway: the market just told you what it wants to believe: Foxconn's real revenue beat outweighed a week of overcapacity theory, and the dip got bought in one session. But a bounce built on one supplier's sales print is a mood, not a verdict. The verdict window is this week: Samsung tonight, Warsh's first minutes Wednesday, Delta kicking off earnings season Friday. 🗓️
  • The signal: notice WHO bounced hardest. Not the megacaps: the picks-and-shovels layer, AMD, Qualcomm, Western Digital, and the datacenter landlords IREN and TeraWulf. When the riskiest end of a trade leads the recovery, that is conviction returning, and it is also exactly where the air comes out fastest if Samsung disappoints. ⚖️
  • The macro: the friendly backdrop is doing quiet work. Jobs have stalled, September hike odds sit near 55%, and OPEC+ keeps pushing oil toward four-month lows, easing the inflation pressure that would force Warsh's hand. A Fed with room to wait is the tide under this whole tape. 🌊
  • TL;DR: the 6-to-12-month bet stays with the broadening: small caps, rate-sensitives, and the other 490 stocks, with chip exposure sized for dispersion, not conviction. This week hands you three real data points. Let the tape earn your belief instead of lending it. 🔒
