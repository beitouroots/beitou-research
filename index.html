📊 Jobs Aftermath

57,000 Jobs Later, the Hike Trade Is Bleeding Out

  • The number: June payrolls rose just 57,000, way under the roughly 110,000 economists expected. Unemployment ticked down to 4.2%, but the rearview stayed ugly: April and May were revised down by a combined 74,000 jobs, and the 12-month average is now a skinny +36,000. 😬
  • Under the hood: leisure and hospitality shed 61,000 jobs on weak summer hiring, health care added 22,000 (slower than its 38,000 monthly average), and wages rose 0.3% on the month, 3.5% on the year, to $37.64 an hour. 🔍
  • The Fed math: per CME FedWatch, odds of a September rate HIKE dimmed to 55% from 64.1% after the print. As 50 Park's Adam Sarhan put it, the report "just takes the pressure off the Fed to raise rates in the short term." 🎲
  • Why it matters: with markets dark today, this is the number the bond market chews on all weekend. Monday's open prices a softer Fed. 🎆
Sources BLS (primary) Reuters CNBC

🏆 The Close

Official Scoreboard: Record Dow, Dead-Flat S&P, and a Tape Split in Two

  • The close: the Dow jumped 594.83 points, +1.14%, to a record 52,900.07. The S&P 500 finished at 7,483.24, up 0.01 points, as flat as a close gets. The Nasdaq fell 0.80% to 25,832.67 and the Russell 2000 slipped 0.55% to 2,996.11. 🏆
  • The streak: that is a fourth straight weekly gain for the Dow, its longest run since October 2024. For the week: Dow +2%, S&P 500 +1.8%, Nasdaq +2.1%. Not bad for a week with a jobs shocker in it. 📈
  • The hero: Apple (AAPL) rose 4.8% and helped carry all three big indexes after Nikkei Asia reported plans for five new iPhone models. Breadth was healthy too: NYSE advancers beat decliners 1.42 to 1. 🍎
  • Why it matters: a record Dow on a soft-jobs day is the market voting for rotation over panic. Everything reopens Monday. 🇺🇸

💾 Semis

The Memory Massacre, Day Two: Chips Fell 5.4% Into the Fireworks

  • The tea: the semiconductor index dropped 5.4% Thursday, its second straight sharp fall. SanDisk (SNDK) sank another 14.1%, Nvidia (NVDA) eased 1.4%, and even after two brutal days the group is still up about 78% in 2026. 💾
  • The why: no earnings miss here. Granite Wealth's Bruce Zaro called it profit-taking after this year's monster run, and Citi put out a warning on semiconductor and hyperscaler names the same day. When everyone has already won, someone starts cashing chips. 🎰
  • The collateral: Tesla (TSLA) dropped 7.5% even though Q2 deliveries came in above estimates, a textbook sell-the-news fade after a sharp run-up into the print. Vimeo owner Bending Spoons fell 11.3% a day after its 40% Nasdaq debut pop. 📉

🛢️ Geopolitics

Crude Just Round-Tripped the Entire War

  • The tape: oil fell for a third straight session Thursday, with WTI at $67.59, down 1.44%, and Brent at $70.60, down 1.36%, in Thursday trade. Brent touched its lowest level since late February: the war premium is basically gone. 🛢️
  • The supply: flows through the Strait of Hormuz are back above 10 million barrels a day, with the UAE restoring exports above 3.9 million bpd and Saudi Arabia ramping shipments to Asia. Meanwhile US and Iranian officials kept talking via Qatari mediators, and Trump said "the denuclearization of Iran is moving along well." 🕊️
  • The Fed angle: Chair Kevin Warsh said Wednesday that "inflation risks have come down," pointing straight at falling energy prices. May CPI ran 4.2%, the hottest since 2023, and crude is unwinding it in real time. 🦅
  • Why it matters: energy is the fastest-moving input into headline inflation. If Hormuz stays open, oil keeps doing the Fed's job for it. ⚖️

🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Barbecue Test: A Market Learning to Rally Without Its Chips

  • The takeaway: this week was the whole regime in miniature. Thursday gave you a record Dow with 70% of the S&P advancing right next to a 5.4% semi wipeout, and then Friday, while America grilled, Seoul snapped back 5.8%. That is volatility inside a rotation, not the start of a crash. 🟣
  • The signal: the market is separating businesses that own AI demand from businesses that merely sell capacity into it. Meta renting out spare compute put the overcapacity question on the table, and Friday's Kospi rip does not answer it, it just proves how fast money floods back at the first hint of a floor. 🔄
  • The macro: payrolls have stalled to a 36,000-a-month average over the past year, inflation is still 4.2%, and oil at four-month lows is doing the Fed's work for it. Per CME FedWatch, September hike odds slid to 55%. A Fed boxed in on both sides leaves breadth and positioning driving the tape. 📊
