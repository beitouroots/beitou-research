🧠 Deep Thoughts

The AI Trade Is Splitting in Two, and a Higher-Rate Fed Is the Judge

  • The big idea: the next 6 to 12 months is no longer "AI up or down." It's a violent rotation within AI: out of expensive software, into the hardware actually printing cash. 🔄
  • The split: even after this week's pullback, the memory names have been the monster winners of 2026, while software king Microsoft just fell into a bear market, off about 21% from its June 1 peak and the worst in the Mag 7. The money is chasing tangible supply, not promises.
  • The squeeze: a Fed whose median dot now points to a 2026 hike raises the bar for every long-duration growth story. Higher-for-longer rates cut the present value of far-off profits, which is exactly what hit the priciest software multiples first. 🦅
  • TL;DR: this looks like a rotation and valuation reset, not a top for the theme. Watch Micron's margins and demand commentary tonight: a sold-out, high-margin HBM book says the hardware boom is real, a soft guide says even the cash-printers got ahead of themselves. 🔒
