🚨 Geopolitics

Round Two: 90 Targets Hit, Three Gulf Bases Answered, One Dead MoU

  • The tea: the US struck Iran for a second straight day. Central Command says it hit about 90 targets Wednesday, after 80 the day before, "to further degrade" Iran's ability to attack commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz. 💥
  • The answer: Tehran fired back at US bases in Bahrain, Kuwait and Qatar, per Iran's semi-official ISNA news agency, and the US Embassy in Jordan issued a security alert overnight. The conflict is spreading across the Gulf, not shrinking. 🌍
  • The politics: the strikes came right after Trump declared the memorandum of understanding with Tehran "is over." US and Iranian delegations had been negotiating a war-ending deal built on that MoU since last month. That scaffolding just collapsed. 📜
  • Why it matters: Bloomberg's framing says it all: fears are growing of a return to full-scale war. Every day this runs, the Hormuz risk premium gets stickier, and it is leaking into everything from tankers to the Fed's inflation math. 🛢️

🛢️ Energy

Oil Keeps Its War Premium. Washington Cuts Off Iran's Barrels

  • The tea: crude is holding Wednesday's rip. WTI settled up 4.4% at $73.52, topped $75 overnight, and sits near $74.20 pre-market. Brent trades near $78.59 after settling at $78.02. The war premium is not fading, it is compounding. 🔥
  • The squeeze: Washington revoked the waiver that let Iran sell its crude, and the strikes followed a string of attacks on vessels in the strait, including a Qatari LNG carrier and a Saudi oil tanker. Supply risk is now policy, not just headlines. 🚢
  • The kicker: a drop in US oil inventories is feeding concern that fuel prices climb from here, and one-year consumer inflation expectations are already sitting at 3.7%. 📈
  • Why it matters: crude in the mid-$70s with an open-ended conflict is exactly the inflation input the Fed cannot model away. Yesterday it was a scare. Today it is a supply regime. 🦅

The Minutes Landed Hawkish. September Is Officially a Hike Watch

  • The tea: Wednesday's minutes from Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Chair confirmed the split everyone feared: 9 of 18 participants projected at least one 2026 hike, 8 saw no change, 1 saw a cut, and Warsh himself abstained from the dots. 📜

  • Why it matters: hawkish minutes plus a live oil shock means the market is now trading a tightening cycle, not an easing one. On deck: Delta (DAL) kicks off earnings season tomorrow before the bell, and the big banks land next week. ⚔️

🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

Priced for a Hike: Investing in the 85% Regime

  • The big idea: the market now puts 85.3% odds on a Fed hike by December, per CME FedWatch. Read that again. The 2026 conversation has flipped from "when do they cut" to "how many hikes," and when the rate regime flips, asset leadership flips with it. 🔄
  • The collision: two inflationary engines are running at once: an energy shock at the world's busiest oil chokepoint, and an AI capex supercycle that prices $28 billion listings above market and signs $19 billion single-tenant data-center leases. The Fed is being asked to tighten into a generational buildout. That has no clean playbook. ⚙️
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, own the toll collectors of both booms: energy producers and services, defense, the memory oligopoly, and the power and data-center landlords. Fund it from long-duration unprofitable growth and rate-sensitive small caps, the assets a hiking Fed punishes first. 📊
  • TL;DR: do not trade the last regime. If December hike odds hold north of 80%, the market's spine is real assets and cash flow today, and every dip in the toll collectors is a gift, not a warning. 🔒

💥 The Standout

Micron Just Promised a Quarter-Trillion Dollars to American Silicon

  • The move: Micron said it will invest more than $250 billion in the US through 2035, up from the $200 billion plan it announced just last June, to feed AI-era memory demand. The stock closed up 7.5% and has gained more than 200% this year. When a memory company raises its own capex bar by fifty billion dollars, that is a demand statement, not a press release. 💾

🧠 The Big Brain · the bottom line
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Market Just Told You It Prices Capex, Not Conflict

  • The takeaway: ninety targets were struck in Iran overnight and every major index closed green while crude fell 2%. That is not complacency; it is a market drawing a line between headlines and barrels. Until Hormuz supply is actually impaired, war risk trades as a day rental, and today the mediators in Qatar and Pakistan mattered more than the missiles. 🗓️
  • The signal: follow the money that got committed today, not the money that got scared. Micron pledged $250 billion, the Pentagon handed a laser maker a $627 million ceiling, and SK Hynix moved to price a $29 billion New York listing, all in one session. Physical-world capex, memory, defense, and power, is where the earnings growth is being manufactured for the next decade. ⚖️
  • The macro: the 6-to-12-month tension is that this capex boom is inflationary at the margin while the Fed is openly split, with a hike camp forming. The 10-year at 4.58% says the bond market has not picked a side yet. If oil stays tame, the capex trade runs; if Hormuz actually closes, everything above gets repriced in an afternoon. Position for the first, respect the second. 🌊
  • TL;DR: keep the barbell: the toll collectors of the AI and defense buildout on one end, cash-generating quality on the other. Earnings season referees it starting tomorrow: Delta before the bell, Hynix at the opening cross. The tape votes on the whole thesis in about 18 hours. 🔒
