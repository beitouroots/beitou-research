Comcast Blows Itself Up, and Wall Street Loves It

  • The tea: Comcast (CMCSA) is splitting into two public companies, spinning off NBCUniversal and Sky into a new media business. The stock soared more than 22% pre-market, on pace for its best day in over a decade. 🚀
  • The split: the new NBCUniversal gets the theme parks, Universal film and TV studios, NBC, Telemundo, Peacock, Bravo and Sky. The company keeping the Comcast name keeps Xfinity broadband, Xfinity Wireless and Comcast Business. ✂️
  • The brass: co-CEO Mike Cavanagh will run NBCUniversal and former CFO Michael Angelakis becomes CEO of Comcast. The tax-free separation is expected to wrap in about a year, with Comcast holding up to a 19.9% stake for up to a year after. 🎬
  • Why it matters: this is the conglomerate playbook, splitting a slow-growth cable pipe from faster-moving media and parks so each gets valued on its own. When growth is scarce, boards manufacture it with a breakup. 🧩

🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Great Unbundling: Breakups Are the New Growth Story

  • The big idea: Comcast blowing itself into two is not a one-off. With organic growth scarce and money expensive, boards are reaching for the breakup as the fastest way to unlock value, splitting slow legacy cash machines from faster-growing crown jewels. 🧩
  • The pattern: it echoes a decade of conglomerate splits, from GE to Kenvue to Honeywell. When a sprawling company trades at a sum-of-the-parts discount, activist and management math both point to the same answer: separate and re-rate. ✂️
  • The catch: spin-offs are not free money. They carry dis-synergies, stranded costs and standalone debt, and the legacy half (here, the cable pipe) often gets left holding the slower-growth bag. The pop is the easy part; the operating story is the hard part. ⚖️
  • TL;DR: over the next 6 to 12 months, watch for copycats across media, telecom and industrials. In a high-rate world where you cannot grow your way to a higher multiple, you engineer one. The breakup wave is just getting started. 🔒
