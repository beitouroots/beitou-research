🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Era of "The Next Move Is a Cut" Just Ended

  • The big idea: for two years the whole market was positioned for rate cuts. Last week the Fed's median dot quietly flipped to a hike, and 9 of 18 officials want one in 2026. That's a regime change, not a footnote. 🔄
  • The why: with core PCE projected at 3.3% and inflation risks skewed up, a Warsh-led Fed is signaling higher-for-longer, maybe higher-period. The bar to ease is now sky-high.
  • The payoff: that slowly reprices everything, long-duration tech multiples, the small-cap "cuts are coming" trade, housing, and gold. Every hot inflation print pulls October-hike odds forward. 📉
  • TL;DR: the dovish autopilot is off. The one thing that saves the bulls is cheap oil from an Iran deal cooling inflation before the Fed has to act. Watch the prints. 🔒
