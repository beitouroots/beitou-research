Trump Says the Ceasefire Is "Over." Oil Just Went Vertical

🦅 The Fed

Warsh's First Minutes Drop at 11 AM Pacific. Here Is the Cheat Sheet

  • The setup: minutes from the June 16-17 FOMC, Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Chair, land at 2 PM ET. The Fed held rates at 3.50% to 3.75% for a fourth straight meeting, and the June dot plot was a shocker: 9 of 18 policymakers penciled in at least one HIKE for 2026, 8 saw no change, and only 1 saw a cut. 📜
  • The twist: Warsh withheld his own dot, so today's transcript is the committee's only on-record steer on a possible September hike. Markets put September hike odds at roughly 50 to 55%, down from 66% before June's soft jobs report, and price a 72.7% chance rates stay put in July. 🎯

  • Why it matters: hawkish minutes plus reflating oil is the worst-case combo for the soft-landing trade. Also on deck: Levi Strauss (LEVI) reports after today's bell, PepsiCo Thursday, Delta Friday, and the big banks kick off earnings season next week. ⚔️

🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Chokepoint Premium: Energy Security Is the New Macro Trade

  • The big idea: ceasefires that die at a chokepoint do not fully resurrect. Even if this week cools off, sanctions are back on Iranian barrels, tankers are getting hit in a strait that carries roughly a fifth of the world's oil, and shippers now price that risk into every voyage. That premium fades slowly, not instantly. 🛢️
  • The collision: this lands on a Fed whose June dot plot already had 9 of 18 members leaning toward a hike. Oil up 6% in a day feeds exactly the inflation nerves Warsh's committee is watching, so energy shocks now transmit straight into rate expectations. El-Erian's $90-plus scenario is the tail that wags this market. 🦅
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, energy producers, oil services and defense act as the portfolio's insurance policy that pays premiums instead of collecting them. Keep the AI barbell, but let the energy side hedge the hawkish-Fed scenario the bond market is already sniffing at 5% on the 30-year. 📊
  • TL;DR: the question is no longer "does the ceasefire hold." It is "where is the new floor under crude," and whether Brent holding the mid-$70s quietly rewrites the Fed math everyone penciled in this spring. 🔒

🧠 The Big Brain · the bottom line
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Market Stopped Paying for Beats and Started Policing Capex

  • The takeaway: put Monday and Tuesday side by side and you have the whole 2026 AI trade in two frames. Monday, a supplier's sales beat sent the Dow to a record. Tuesday, the best quarter in tech history got sold. The market is no longer paying for proof of demand; it is policing what the winners spend and who might undercut them next. 🗓️
  • The signal: the DeepSeek chip report matters more than its details. When your biggest customers start sketching their own inference silicon, the thing on trial is not AI demand, it is the scarcity premium baked into every chip multiple. Demand can stay historic while the toll rates get renegotiated. ⚖️
  • The macro: the friendly backdrop got a little less friendly. The Hormuz attack put a tail-risk premium back under oil, and firmer crude feeds exactly the inflation nerves that make a September hike livelier. Tomorrow's minutes from Warsh's first meeting tell you how twitchy the new Fed really is. 🌊
  • TL;DR: the 6-to-12-month bet stays with the broadening: small caps, rate-sensitives, and the other 490 stocks, with chip exposure sized for dispersion, not conviction. Rotation days like today are the broadening thesis cashing its first checks. 🔒
