📊 Jobs Day

America Added Just 57,000 Jobs, and the Market Said Thank You

  • The number: June payrolls rose just 57,000, a big miss against the roughly 100,000 to 115,000 economists expected. Unemployment ticked down to 4.2%, but the ugly part is the rearview: April was revised down to +148,000 and May to +129,000, a combined 74,000 jobs erased. The 12-month average is now a skinny +36,000. 😬
  • Under the hood: leisure and hospitality shed 61,000 jobs on weak summer hiring, while professional services (+36,000), social assistance (+25,000) and health care (+22,000) did the lifting. Participation slipped 0.3 point to 61.5%, and wages rose 0.3% on the month, 3.5% on the year. 🔍
  • The reaction: stocks LIKED it. Futures flipped green (S&P +0.29%, Dow +0.42% pre-market), the 2-year yield eased and gold jumped about 2%. In this tape, soft jobs data cools the rate-HIKE chatter, so bad news is good news. 🟢
  • Why it matters: this print landed a day early because markets are closed tomorrow, Friday July 3, for the July 4th holiday. Today is the last word before the Fed's July meeting chatter takes over. 🎆
Sources BLS (primary) CNBC Kiplinger
02
🦅 The Fed

Warsh Says Prices Are Too High. The Jobs Data Just Talked Back

  • The tea: Fed Chair Kevin Warsh told the ECB's Sintra forum Wednesday that "we've all looked around, and we've seen that prices are too high," calling inflation the Fed's primary objective while declining to hint at the July decision. Yields rose on the remarks. 🦅
  • The hawk count: Minneapolis Fed's Neel Kashkari has already flipped, saying he has one rate HIKE penciled in for 2026, up from one cut in March, citing broad inflationary pressures beyond the Middle East. 📈
  • The odds: heading into this morning, markets priced roughly 73% odds the Fed holds in July and about 65% odds of a quarter-point hike by September. Then payrolls printed +57,000, and the 2-year yield eased as hike bets faded. 🎲
  • Why it matters: a Fed talking about hikes into a labor market averaging +36,000 jobs a month is the whole second-half tension in one sentence. Something has to give. ⚔️
Sources CNBC Bloomberg FXStreet

🤖 Tech

Meta Is Now a Cloud Company. The Memory Trade Got Smoked

  • The tea: Meta (META) popped 8.8% to close at $612.91 Wednesday after unveiling a cloud push to sell its excess AI compute capacity to outside customers. All those GPUs Zuckerberg hoarded are becoming a revenue line, and suddenly Meta is talking like a rival to the AWS and Azure crowd. ☁️
  • The flip side: memory got wrecked. Micron (MU) fell 10.6% to $1,032.28 and SanDisk (SNDK) dropped 10.6% to $2,032.22, with Western Digital down about 7%, as traders banked massive gains: MU came in up 305% year to date and SNDK up 858% through Tuesday. 💾
  • The kicker: a California class action alleges Samsung, SK Hynix and Micron coordinated to restrict DRAM supply, and research shop Citrini warned big buyers may start economizing on memory after the price explosion. Nvidia (NVDA) eased about 1%. ⚖️
  • Why it matters: a hyperscaler with spare compute to SELL is the first real crack in the "infinite AI demand" story. Watch what that does to the whole capex food chain. 🔌
Sources CNBC 24/7 Wall St Invezz

🛢️ Geopolitics

Doha Diplomacy Is Crushing Crude

  • The tea: Qatar's foreign ministry says the US and Iran made positive progress in indirect talks in Doha, with envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner working through Qatari mediators. Trump says the talks are going well. Oil keeps sliding: WTI is down 1.2% to $67.75 and Brent off 1.1% to $70.82 pre-market. 🕊️
  • The wild backdrop: this diplomacy comes days after a flare-up threatened the 60-day ceasefire, with Iran attacking two commercial vessels and the US striking targets inside Iran in response. The market is choosing to price the peace, not the pyrotechnics. 💥
  • The scale: the war premium is just about gone. Brent fell nearly 40% this quarter, its worst quarter since 2020, after trading above $126 as recently as late April. ING says traders are betting Persian Gulf supply normalizes. 📉
  • Why it matters: crude back near pre-war levels is a giant disinflation tailwind. Cheap oil quietly does the inflation-fighting the Fed keeps talking about. The one variable that reprices everything: the Strait of Hormuz. ⚖️
Sources CNBC CNN CNBC Africa

🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Hike That Never Comes: Oil Is Doing the Fed's Job

  • The big idea: the market's biggest fight over the next 6 to 12 months is Warsh's hawkish talk versus the data. Brent has collapsed from above $126 in late April to about $71 today, and energy is the fastest-moving input into headline inflation. If Doha holds, the disinflation impulse through year-end is enormous. 🛢️
  • The other blade: the labor market is stalling in plain sight: +57,000 in June, a 12-month average of +36,000, and participation at 61.5%. Central banks do not usually hike into that. The bet: the 2026 rate hike never actually arrives. 🦅
  • The trade: if hike odds keep bleeding, duration, small caps and rate-sensitives are the winners, and the Russell 2000 near records suggests the market is already sniffing it out. The Big Brain leans that way. 📊
  • TL;DR: two risks can wreck it: the Strait of Hormuz reigniting, or tariff-driven prices forcing Warsh's hand. Absent those, the hawks are writing checks the data will not cash. 🔒

🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Great Re-Sort: Who Actually Owns the AI Trade

  • The takeaway: today was two markets in one. A record Dow with 70% of the S&P advancing on one screen, and a global AI-infrastructure unwind on the other, from Micron to Seoul. That is not a crash, that is a re-sort. 🟣

The market is now separating businesses that own AI demand from businesses that merely sell capacity into it, and the memory names that went parabolic are the first casualties of that question. 🔄
  • The macro: jobs growth has stalled to a 36,000-a-month average over the past year while oil sits at four-month lows doing the Fed's inflation-fighting for it. A Fed that can neither hike nor cut leaves breadth and positioning driving the tape, and right now the money is broadening out. 📊
