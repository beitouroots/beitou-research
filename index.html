🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Hormuz Premium Is Becoming a Permanent Line Item

  • The big idea: this is now a recurring show: strikes, a closure threat, an oil spike, a partial cool-down, repeat. When a chokepoint that carries about 20% of the world's oil gets threatened this often, the risk premium stops being an event and starts being a feature. Price it like a subscription, not a one-time charge. 🔁
  • The physics: the strait does not have to actually close to matter. Insurance, freight and rerouting costs rise on the threat alone, and every producer, refiner and utility starts paying up for supply security. That is structural demand for energy that never touches Hormuz. 🚢
  • The trade: over 6 to 12 months, watch the energy security complex: US LNG exporters, pipeline capacity that bypasses the strait, tanker rates, and plain old US shale optionality. In a world where the Fed's next move might be a hike, energy is quietly reclaiming its old job as portfolio insurance. 🛡️
  • TL;DR: Friday we said memory is the new oil. This weekend, oil stood up and reminded everyone it is still the old oil. Own some insurance. 🛢️

💥 The Standout

SK Hynix Just Pulled Off the Biggest Foreign IPO in US History

  • The record: the South Korean memory giant raised $26.5 billion in its US share offering, the largest first-time listing by a foreign company in American history, topping the $25 billion Alibaba raised back in 2014. The deal: 177.9 million American depositary receipts at $149 each, with each ADR representing one-tenth of a common share, and demand reported at about seven times the shares available. 🏆
  • The debut: shares opened at $170, a 14% jump, and closed the session roughly 13% higher near $168. They traded Friday under the conditional ticker SKHYV and switch to the regular SKHY ticker on Monday. For a listing billed all week as a referendum on the AI trade, the verdict came back fast: still hungry. 📈
  • Why it matters: SK Hynix is the key high-bandwidth memory supplier to Nvidia, and the proceeds are earmarked for AI memory capacity. One day after Micron pledged more than $250 billion to US fabs, Wall Street just handed the other memory heavyweight $26.5 billion, and US officials are already urging it to build American fabs too. The memory arms race is now fully bankrolled. 🇺🇸🇰🇷
