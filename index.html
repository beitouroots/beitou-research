🦅 The Fed

Fed Chair Warsh Heads Back to Congress: "The Inflation Fight Isn't Over"

  • The tea: Fed Chair Kevin Warsh testifies before the Senate Banking Committee today at 10 AM ET, the second leg of his first semiannual Monetary Policy Report to Congress after his House testimony yesterday. He became the 17th Fed Chair on May 22, 2026. 🎙️
  • The message: Warsh told lawmakers yesterday that the recent improvement in inflation isn't "mission accomplished," outlined five task forces reviewing factors affecting monetary policy, and said any changes to the Fed's $6.7 trillion balance sheet would be telegraphed in advance. 📋
  • The backdrop: he's speaking hours after cooler June CPI and PPI knocked the odds of a July hike down toward the mid-teens, per CME FedWatch, a sharp reversal from the roughly 46.5% hike odds priced in earlier this week as oil spiked on the Iran conflict. 😳
  • Why it matters: Sen. Elizabeth Warren is expected to press Warsh on his unemployment and inflation expectations. How he balances the cooling data against a resurgent oil shock will shape whether markets keep pricing this as a hold. ⚖️

🤖 Tech

ASML Raises Guidance for the Second Time This Year, and Chip Stocks Are Loving It

  • The tea: ASML (ASML) beat Q2 estimates, with revenue up more than 6% to €9.33 billion against a roughly €8.80 billion consensus, and net income of €2.92 billion versus a €2.62 billion estimate. US-listed shares jumped 5.2% premarket. 🔬

📊 Macro

Inflation Just Cooled Way More Than Expected, and Rate-Hike Odds Cratered With It

  • The tea: June CPI fell 0.4% month over month, a bigger drop than expected, bringing annual inflation down to 3.5% versus a 3.8% forecast, while core CPI held flat. 🌶️
  • The follow-through: June PPI fell 0.3% against a forecast for no change, with the annual rate cooling to 5.5% from May's 6.0%. Goods prices dropped 1.4% (the biggest monthly drop since July 2022) as gasoline tumbled 12%, though core PPI still rose 0.2%. 📉
  • The odds shift: after the PPI print, CME FedWatch showed the odds of the Fed holding rates steady in July climbing back above 85%, reversing much of the hike bet that built up earlier this week on the oil spike. 🎢
  • Why it matters: this is the softest back-to-back inflation read in months, but it landed as oil is climbing again on the Iran conflict (Story 1), setting up a genuine tug-of-war for how Warsh frames today's testimony. ⚖️

🧠 The Big Brain · the 6-12 month bet
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The Fed's Next Move Is Now a Hostage to the Oil Price, Not Just the CPI Print

  • The big idea: the market just watched hike odds swing from roughly 46% earlier this week to under 15% this morning, not because the underlying growth or jobs picture changed, but because oil went up, then a data print came in soft. That kind of volatility in rate expectations, driven by a supply shock instead of demand, is a preview of Warsh's whole tenure. 🎢
  • The tension: Warsh has already signaled he wants to run a hawkish Fed after "five years of elevated inflation," but a sustained Hormuz conflict could hand him a textbook supply-side inflation problem, the kind rate hikes don't actually fix and can make worse by slowing growth into a cost-push shock. 🛢️
  • The trade: over the next 6 to 12 months, watch the Iran conflict as closely as the FOMC calendar. If it drags on or escalates, Warsh will face real pressure to choose between fighting headline inflation and risking a policy mistake into an energy-driven growth slowdown. 🔬
  • TL;DR: today's story is CPI, PPI, ASML and a PayPal buyout, but the trade that could still matter next summer is whether Washington's own inflation fighter gets outmaneuvered by a barrel of oil. 🔒

🧠 The Big Brain · the bottom line
🧠 Deep Thoughts

The AI Trade Is Splitting Into Winners and Losers Inside Its Own Supply Chain

  • The takeaway: today was not a referendum on AI, it was a referendum on which parts of the AI stack win. Apple's China approval and Alibaba's Qwen deal show the consumer-facing platform layer of AI still has real room to run, while Micron's slide on Chinese memory competition shows the hardware supply chain is getting genuinely contested by overseas rivals, not just US peers. 🔬
  • The tension: cooling wholesale inflation gives the Fed room to stay patient, but that macro tailwind is now competing with company-specific and country-specific stories, China's regulatory posture toward Apple on one side, China's memory-chip capacity on the other, that have nothing to do with rate policy. ⚖️
  • The trade: watch whether Micron's slide is a one-day scare or the start of a real re-rating of US memory makers against Chinese competition. That debate matters more to the semiconductor trade over the next 6 to 12 months than this week's inflation print. 🔍
  • TL;DR: Big Tech and cooling inflation carried the indices to fresh highs today, but underneath the surface, AI's supply chain just showed investors it has real geographic risk, and SpaceX's slide below its IPO price is a reminder that hype alone does not hold up a stock price forever. 🔒
